What is Beanstock

Beanstock Coffee Festival is a single-day event to showcase the best independent micro-roasters and celebrate third wave coffee culture. Held in a bright and spacious Railtown warehouse, the immersive experience will exhibit music, local food and drink selections, educational sessions from industry professionals, live demos, unlimited coffee sampling, and a curated market to purchase equipment and coffee to take home. Entry-level tickets provide unlimited coffee samples, a commemorative mug and a ticket for a sample bag of beans from your micro-roaster of choice. Premium tickets also include vouchers for specialty food items, a custom tote bag and two additional bags of coffee beans.

What is Third Wave Coffee

Third Wave Coffee is a movement to produce high-quality coffee, and consider coffee as an artisanal product, like wine, rather than a commodity. Fun fact: there are over 1,500 aromatic and flavour compounds in coffee. Wine has less than half of that.

Every Coffee Has A Story

It all begins with a fruit.

  • GROW

    Coffee is grown at different origins along the equator, each with their own harvest periods and unique flavours. After a coffee tree is planted, in about three to four years the tree begins to bear fruit - the coffee cherry. Inside this cherry is the bean that we all know and love, but it still has a long way to go.

  • PICK

    Almost all coffee in the world is hand picked. A good coffee picker can harvest about two hundred pounds of coffee in a day. The cherries can only be harvested during one season of the year. So many people owe their livelihood to a good harvest year.

  • PROCESS

    After the cherries are picked and sorted, they are processed in a number of different ways to remove the cherry and parchment surrounding the bean. The processing of the bean can drastically change the way it tastes, so an alarming amount of skill and science is involved in this process. What you are left with is known in the industry as ‘green.’

  • EXPORT

    Once the green beans are properly dried and sorted, they are put into burlap or jute bags, loaded up onto trucks and prepared for export to the rest of the world.

  • ROAST

    Roasting coffee is an art in itself, taking countless years to master and even then there is always more to learn. Each roaster has their own unique techniques,manipulating heat, airflow, and time to produce different roast levels and bring out different flavours in coffee. Getting a sense for how delicate the coffee process is?

  • PREPARE

    This is the final step in the lifecycle of a coffee and the only stage that most of us ever experience.
